The Home

You pull up to 176 N Mills Rd and immediately sense this isn't the typical midtown Ventura bungalow. There's something about the way the home sits on the lot — confident, established, quietly commanding attention. The paver driveway stretches long enough to accommodate an RV, a rare find in this neighborhood, and hints at the thoughtful updates waiting inside.

Step through the front door and the main floor unfolds with the warmth of a classic California cottage. Three bedrooms and two full baths occupy this level, including a walk-in curbless shower that speaks to both elegance and accessibility. The kitchen anchors the heart of the home — granite countertops, refreshed cabinetry, recessed lighting that keeps the space bright without ever feeling harsh. It's the kind of kitchen where morning coffee feels like a ritual and casual dinners stretch into long conversations.

But the real revelation happens upstairs. The primary suite isn't just a bedroom — it's a retreat. Vaulted tongue-and-groove wood ceilings draw the eye upward, exposed beams adding character that can't be manufactured. Natural light pours in from multiple angles, giving the space an airy, treehouse-like quality. Then you step onto the balcony, and there they are: the Channel Islands, framed perfectly against the Pacific horizon. This is the view that stops conversations. The view that makes you realize you're not just buying square footage — you're buying a daily experience of Ventura at its finest.

The primary bath matches the moment: travertine tiled shower, jetted Roman tub, dual sinks, and a wraparound walk-in closet that feels more like a dressing room than an afterthought. Every detail suggests intention — someone designed this space to be lived in and loved.
